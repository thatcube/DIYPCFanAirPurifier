ARGB Wiring

Step 1 — Split the 12V supply

The DC splitter takes one PSU output and splits it:

  • Output A → PWM fan adapter → powers all fan motors at 12V
  • Output B → step-down module → converts 12V to 5V

Step 2 — Power the ESP32

The step-down module’s USB-C output plugs directly into the ESP32-C6’s USB-C port. Done.

Step 3 — Get 5V to the ARGB LEDs

The ESP32’s 5V/VIN pin exposes the USB bus voltage. The step-down is rated 3A; the LEDs across 6 fans draw ~0.3A — plenty of headroom.

Buy a 5V 3-pin ARGB extension cable (~$3). Cut it in the middle. The loose end has 3 wires:

  • Red (5V) → step-down output pads (or ESP32 5V pin as fallback)
  • Black (GND) → any GND pin on ESP32 or step-down
  • Data (green/white) → through 74AHCT125 level shifter → ESP32 GPIO. The chip guarantees a clean 5V signal. Try without first — often works.

Use WAGO lever connectors for solder-free joins.

Step 4 — Daisy-chain the fans

Each P12 Pro has ARGB in and out ports. Chain like Christmas lights:

[cable] → fan 1 in … out → fan 2 in … out → fan 3 …

One GPIO pin controls all 6 fans’ colors at once.

Step 5 — Daisy-chain power/PWM (PST)

Same idea — chain all fans per panel via PST. One PWM signal controls speed for all. Route the cable between panels through the interior void, zip-tied along the bottom.

Filter Retention

The left and right sides are open — no side panels. Filters slide straight in.

  1. Glue & nail ~½"×⅜" wood strips along each side edge of the top and bottom panels (4 strips total). They run ~70% of the panel depth, centered.
  2. Strips sit inboard so filters rest against them, roughly flush with the exterior.
  3. A continuous foam tape ring (~½" × 3/32") seals all 4 inner panel faces.
  4. Filters are held by ledge strips above/below and foam compression. Slide in from the side.

Firmware

Flash ESPHome to the ESP32-C6:

  • fan component → PWM duty cycle for speed control
  • light component with WS2812B driver → ARGB chain
  • HomeKit via Home Assistant, or native Matter (C6 supports Matter without a hub on iOS 16.2+)

Gives you Siri speed control, RGB scenes, and full Home app integration.

Build Order

1Cut all 4 plywood panels (top: 7.0"×25.69", bottom: same, front: 7.0"×19.69", back: same)
2Cut fan openings in front & back panels (3 × 120mm circles, evenly spaced, 1.5" buffer top/bottom)
3Glue & assemble the box — top, bottom, front, back. Sides left open for filters
4Install ledge strips on inner faces of top & bottom panels (2 per panel, along each side edge)
5Apply foam tape around all 4 inner panel faces as a continuous seal ring
6Mount fans (3 per panel); daisy-chain PST cables
7Run ARGB & PWM cables through interior; zip-tie along bottom panel
8Wire electronics: PSU, step-down, ESP32 per wiring plan above
9Flash ESPHome to ESP32-C6, configure fan + light entities
10Slide filters in from the sides, test airflow & ARGB
11Attach feet to bottom panel corners
